CODE RED: PERIOD POVERTY
SPEAKER PANEL



Join us on June 5th, 2023 for our panel!

Featuring Nabilah Islam, Jess Strait, Nadya Okamoto, and other incredible individuals as we open space to talk about period poverty in relation to work, government, policy, sustainability and more! This panel is free and virtual. Scroll down to get more information on registration!

The goal of this panel is to spread awareness, open up a safe-space for the discussion of periods, share experiences, and ultimately support all menstruating individuals, regardless of age, sex, class, race, location, appearance, or orientation.

FEATURED GUESTS

Lina Lyte Plioplyte

Emmy, Clio, and Silver Lion winning director and camera person. Lina's documentaries explore themes regarding the championing of women, ranging from stigmas regarding aging to menstruation. She's also the Director of Periodical which “tells the unexpected story of the human body by exploring the marvel and mystery of the menstrual cycle, from the first period to the last.” The periodical features interviews from a large group of women, ranging from soccer star Megan Rapinoe to scientists discovering the possibilities of stem cells in period blood.



Jess Strait

Founder of Days for Girls International Club at Penn State University, working to increase access to menstrual care and education by developing global partnerships, cultivating Social Enterprises, mobilizing volunteers and innovating sustainable solutions that shatter stigma and limitations for women and girls. Her work there included leading a campus-wide menstrual cup distribution, co-instructing a course on menstrual equity, developing a menstrual health needs analysis, and running sewing operations for the club. Her undergraduate thesis also focused on period stigma among college students.



Nadya Okamoto

Founder of PERIOD (period.org), an organization fighting to end period poverty. PERIOD addressed over 1.5 million periods and registered over 800 campus chapters in all 50 states and 50 other countries. Co-founder of August, a lifestyle brand working to reimagine periods. Published a book titled Period Power: A Manifesto for the Menstrual Movement, which made Kirkus Reviews list for Best Young Adult Nonfiction of 2018. Creator of the podcast TIGRESS. Leading speaker about menstrual justice, the power of Generation Z, and mental health.



Nabilah Islam

Member of the Georgia State Senate since 2023, Islam is focused on quality healthcare access and Medicaid expansion. She is the daughter of working-class immigrants from Bangladesh, ultimately dedicating her life and career to advancing progressive ideals in Georgia. Recently, Islam introduced and championed GA SB 51: a bill to exempt menstrual products from the Georgia state sales and use tax, heard in the GA Senate Finance Committee February 6th, 2023, – a vital piece of menstrual equity legislation.



Umyeena Bashir

The founder and CEO of the period tracking app spotLight. She also works for WVE (women’s voices for the earth) and is studying the toxic labels and chemicals used in period care products. She also participates in outreach research and has written on topics like the lack of research and education on periods and how that affects women’s health.



Lena Vann

The founder and director of the Black Period(t) Project. She won the 2021 Pink With purpose project. Her organization works towards guaranteeing that all Black women, girls and trans men have access to menstruation and hygiene products. The organization is a non-profit based in North Carolina. The Black Period(t) Project creates menstruation kits for youth shelters, title I schools and homeless shelters. They also push for legislation that allows all peoples to bleed with dignity and provides age-appropriate menstruation education.
